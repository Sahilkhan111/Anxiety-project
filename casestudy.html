Case Studies

A guide to recognizing, understanding, and managing anxiety.

Case Study 1: Sarah Khan

  • Name: Sarah Khan
  • Age: 32 years
  • Occupation: Marketing Executive at a Tech Firm
  • Presenting Issue: Excessive worry about work performance and social interactions.
  • Duration of Symptoms: 6 months
  • Medical History: No previous psychiatric disorders. Generally healthy.

Sarah experienced overwhelming anxiety primarily related to her job, including fear of failure, worry over social interactions, and excessive self-criticism. Her anxiety began affecting her sleep, work performance, and social relationships.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Sarah was diagnosed with Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD). She underwent Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and was prescribed sertraline. After 12 weeks, Sarah reported significant improvement in her anxiety symptoms, including better sleep, improved work focus, and a reduction in social avoidance.

Case Study 2: Tom Edwards

  • Name: Tom Edwards
  • Age: 28 years
  • Occupation: Software Developer
  • Presenting Issue: Extreme fear of social interactions and public speaking.
  • Duration of Symptoms: 2 years
  • Medical History: No significant medical issues. Previous episodes of panic attacks.

Tom struggled with intense anxiety when interacting with others, particularly in social settings and professional environments. He often avoided gatherings, conferences, or even casual meetings, fearing embarrassment and judgment.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Tom was diagnosed with Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD). He participated in exposure therapy, a form of CBT designed to gradually confront feared social situations. Additionally, he was prescribed a beta-blocker to help manage physical symptoms of anxiety during social interactions. Over several months, Tom gained more confidence in social settings and gradually reduced his avoidance behaviors.

Case Study 3: Maria Rodriguez

  • Name: Maria Rodriguez
  • Age: 36 years
  • Occupation: Teacher
  • Presenting Issue: Sudden and recurrent panic attacks, especially in crowded places.
  • Duration of Symptoms: 1 year
  • Medical History: No prior psychiatric conditions. Recently experienced a traumatic event.

Maria began experiencing panic attacks, often feeling like she was choking or unable to breathe. These attacks typically occurred in public places, such as grocery stores or while traveling, leaving her anxious about having another panic attack in public.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Maria was diagnosed with Panic Disorder. She was treated with a combination of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and medications such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) to reduce the frequency and intensity of panic attacks. After six months of treatment, Maria experienced a notable decrease in panic episodes and regained control over her life.

Case Study 4: David Brooks

  • Name: David Brooks
  • Age: 24 years
  • Occupation: Graphic Designer
  • Presenting Issue: Intense fear of flying (aviophobia) impacting work and personal life.
  • Duration of Symptoms: 4 years
  • Medical History: No history of anxiety disorders.

David had a severe fear of flying, which severely limited his ability to travel for both work and personal reasons. His anxiety about flights was triggered by the fear of being in an enclosed space and experiencing turbulence.

Diagnosis and Treatment

David was diagnosed with a Specific Phobia (aviophobia). He underwent exposure therapy, which involved gradually confronting the idea of flying, starting with viewing planes and progressing to sitting in a flight simulator. Over time, David's fear diminished significantly, allowing him to fly without experiencing extreme anxiety.

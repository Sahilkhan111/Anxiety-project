What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a natural response to stress, characterized by feelings of worry, nervousness, or unease. It can affect daily life when persistent or excessive.

  • American Psychological Association (APA): Anxiety is an emotion characterized by feelings of tension, worried thoughts, and physical changes like increased blood pressure.
  • Mayo Clinic: Anxiety is a normal part of life that involves feelings of fear or apprehension about whatâ€™s to come.
  • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH): Anxiety refers to the anticipation of a future concern and is more associated with muscle tension and avoidance behavior.

Symptoms of Anxiety

Physical Symptoms

Increased heart rate, sweating, shaking, and shortness of breath.

Emotional Symptoms

Feelings of dread, irritability, or a sense of impending doom.

Behavioral Symptoms

Avoiding situations that trigger anxiety and restlessness.

Why Does Anxiety Happen?

Anxiety can stem from a combination of genetic, environmental, and psychological factors. Stressful life events, imbalanced brain chemistry, and inherited traits are common contributors.
